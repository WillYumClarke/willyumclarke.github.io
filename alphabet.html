Alphabet Reordering

Overview

Something that caught my eye recently was an experiment Brian Kell did back in 2010, he made an attempt to find what order you could put the alphabet into to yield the most English words as substrings (a substring is a smaller word present in a larger word, like the word sub is a substring of substring).

His page walking through his experiment can be found here: Brian Kell

Size of Experiment

Alphabet

The first thing to consider in this experiment is how many different reordering’s of the alphabet you can possible make, and it’s quite simple, 26!, or 403,291,461,126,605,635,584,000,000, or 4.0329146e+26. However you write it, the number of possible combinations is impossible to fathom and it would be impossible to generate all possible combinations before the sun swallows up the earth. Given this fact, along with searching for both the reordering with the most and least words, I see no point in attempting to test reordering’s in any sort of order or sensical fashion and instead using mans age old best friend, random chance.
